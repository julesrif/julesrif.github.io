Launch into Computing serves as a comprehensive introduction to core computing disciplines, including Software Engineering, AI, Cybersecurity, Data Science, and Enterprise IT Management. Over 12 weeks, students explore fundamental principles, methodologies, and emerging trends, building a strong theoretical foundation while engaging in practical applications relevant to real-world enterprise challenges.

## Learning Outcomes

1. Identify and critically analyse computing challenges and processes in business systems, accounting for the current enterprise landscape.
2. Gather and synthesise information from multiple sources (including internet resources and business publications) to aid in the systematic design and analysis of computing challenges.
3. Critically evaluate appropriate methodologies, tools and techniques to mitigate and/or solve computing issues and their business impact.
4. Articulate the legal, social, ethical, and professional issues faced by computing professionals.

---

## Key Artefacts

- **Collaborative Discussion:** AI and RAG-based semantic search applied to chemical plant maintenance - exploring how Retrieval Augmented Generation can bridge knowledge gaps in industrial operations.
- **Reflective Practice:** Comparative reflection between local and cloud storage models for data science projects.
- **Final Essay:** Evaluating the future of computing - AI governance, ethics, and the role of GDPR in responsible innovation.
- **Programming Project:** AES-128 file encryption tool comparing Python and C++ implementations (performance, security, maintainability).
